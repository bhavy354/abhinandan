Zeodary Roots

Description

Zeodary roots, also known as Curcuma zedoaria, are aromatic rhizomes with a bitter and pungent taste. Our premium quality zeodary roots are carefully harvested, cleaned, and dried to preserve their essential oils and medicinal properties. These roots have a pale yellow interior with a distinct earthy aroma.

100% natural with no additives
Rich in essential oils and curcuminoids
Long shelf life (up to 2 years)
Retains original flavor and medicinal properties

Specifications

Color: Pale yellow to brown

Moisture: Max 10%

Size: 5-10cm pieces

Packaging: 10kg, 25kg, 50kg bags

Storage Instructions

Store in a cool, dry place away from direct sunlight. Keep in airtight containers to maintain freshness and prevent moisture absorption. Ideal storage temperature is below 25°C with relative humidity below 65%. Avoid storing near strong-smelling substances as the roots can absorb odors.

Culinary & Medicinal Uses

Zeodary roots have been used for centuries in traditional medicine and culinary applications. They are valued for their:

  • Flavoring agent in traditional Asian cuisine
  • Essential ingredient in Ayurvedic and Chinese medicine
  • Digestive aid and appetite stimulant
  • Anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties
  • Natural remedy for respiratory ailments
  • Ingredient in herbal teas and tonics

Industrial & Other Uses

Beyond culinary and medicinal applications, zeodary roots have several industrial uses:

  • Essential oil extraction for perfumery
  • Natural food coloring agent
  • Ingredient in cosmetic preparations
  • Flavoring in alcoholic beverages

Origin & Cultivation

Zeodary root is native to Southeast Asia and has been cultivated for thousands of years for its medicinal properties. Modern zeodary root products are produced from high-quality plants grown in optimal conditions.

Growing Regions

The best quality zeodary roots come from these regions:

  • Northeast India - known for high essential oil content
  • Indonesia - traditional cultivation areas
  • Thailand - produces premium quality roots
  • Bangladesh - wild and cultivated varieties
  • South China - used in traditional Chinese medicine

Processing Method

Our processing method preserves maximum medicinal properties:

  1. Selection of mature 2-3 year old plants
  2. Careful harvesting to preserve rhizomes
  3. Thorough cleaning and washing
  4. Precision cutting to required sizes
  5. Controlled drying at optimal temperatures
  6. Quality testing and sorting
  7. Packaging in moisture-proof materials
