Wheat

Description

Wheat is one of the world's most important cereal crops, providing a staple food for a large part of the global population. Our premium wheat grains are carefully selected for their high quality, nutritional value, and excellent milling properties. Wheat is rich in carbohydrates, proteins, dietary fiber, and essential minerals.

High in protein and dietary fiber
Excellent milling quality
Non-GMO and naturally grown
Rich in B vitamins and minerals

Specifications

Color: Golden brown to amber

Moisture: Max 12%

Protein Content: 10-14%

Packaging: 25kg, 50kg bags or bulk

Storage Instructions

Store in a cool, dry, and well-ventilated area away from direct sunlight. Maintain temperature below 25°C and relative humidity below 65%. Keep away from pests and rodents. For long-term storage, use airtight containers or silos with proper fumigation.

Culinary Uses

Wheat is a versatile grain with numerous culinary applications across various cuisines worldwide:

  • Production of wheat flour for bread, pasta, noodles, and pastries
  • Whole wheat grains used in porridges, pilafs, and salads
  • Breakfast cereals and muesli mixes
  • Bulgur wheat for tabbouleh and other Middle Eastern dishes
  • Wheat bran as a dietary fiber supplement
  • Wheat germ as a nutritional additive

Industrial & Other Uses

Beyond food applications, wheat has several industrial and commercial uses:

  • Animal feed and livestock nutrition
  • Production of biofuels and ethanol
  • Manufacturing of wheat-based adhesives
  • Cosmetic and personal care products
  • Pharmaceutical applications as a binder

Origin & Cultivation

Wheat is one of the first cereals to be domesticated, with its origins tracing back to the Fertile Crescent around 10,000 years ago. Today, it's cultivated worldwide and is the second most-produced cereal after maize.

Growing Regions

The major wheat producing regions include:

  • Punjab and Haryana, India - known for high-quality wheat
  • Great Plains, USA - large-scale commercial production
  • Ukraine and Russia - major global exporters
  • Canada - known for premium hard red spring wheat
  • Australia - drought-resistant varieties

Cultivation Process

Wheat cultivation follows these key steps:

  1. Land preparation with proper soil conditioning
  2. Sowing of certified seeds at optimal density
  3. Application of balanced fertilizers and irrigation
  4. Pest and disease management
  5. Harvesting at proper moisture content
  6. Threshing, cleaning, and proper storage

Quality Standards

Our wheat meets international quality standards including FSSAI, APEDA, and USDA specifications. We maintain strict quality control measures for moisture content, foreign matter, damaged grains, and other quality parameters throughout the supply chain.
