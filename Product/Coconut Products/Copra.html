Copra

Description

Copra is the dried kernel of mature coconuts, mainly used for extracting coconut oil. It is a vital raw material for the edible oil industry, as well as in food, cosmetics, and animal feed applications. Copra is available in various forms such as ball copra, cup copra, and milling copra, depending on its intended use. Known for its high oil content and rich aroma, copra is a cornerstone product in the coconut value chain.

Storage Instructions

Store in an airtight container in a cool, dry place away from direct sunlight. Ideal storage temperature: 15°C to 25°C. Shelf life: Up to 12 months in proper conditions.

Culinary Uses

  • Oil Extraction: Primary source for cold-pressed and refined coconut oil.
  • Traditional Cooking: Used in South Indian curries, gravies, and coconut-based dishes after rehydrating.
  • Sweets & Confectionery: Used in preparation of coconut burfis, laddoos, and other desserts.
  • Bakery Industry: Occasionally ground into coarse flour for specialty recipes.
  • Desserts & Ice Creams: Adds a crunchy texture to puddings, parfaits, and ice creams.
  • Used in curries, gravies, and chutneys for flavor & texture
  • Coconut Milk Production: Copra is processed for thick coconut milk extraction.

Medicinal & Other Uses

  • Rich in Dietary Fiber: Supports digestion and gut health.
  • Natural Source of Good Fats: Contains MCTs for quick energy metabolism.
  • Ayurveda & Natural Remedies: Known for cooling properties and mild laxative effects.
  • By-Product Uses: Flake residue (after oil extraction) is used in animal feed industry.

Copra Varieties

We offer several varieties of Copra:

  • Cup Copra (Half-cut Copra): Dried half shells, commonly used for domestic cooking and puja rituals.
  • Milling Copra: Used primarily for oil extraction in industrial processes.
  • Ball Copra: Fully dried, spherical copra used for edible purposes & temple offerings.

Origin & Cultivation

  • Primarily sourced from coastal regions of South India (Tamil Nadu, Kerala, Karnataka)
  • Grown in tropical climates with abundant rainfall and rich sandy loam soils
  • Harvested after 12 months of planting
  • Coconuts are de-husked, shelled, pared, and then shredded and dried to achieve fine texture
  • Strict quality checks ensure food safety compliance for international markets
