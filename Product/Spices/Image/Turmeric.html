Turmeric Finger

Description

Our premium Turmeric Finger is sourced directly from the finest farms in India, known for producing the highest quality turmeric with vibrant color and rich aroma. These whole turmeric rhizomes are carefully selected, cleaned, and sun-dried to preserve their natural goodness.

Turmeric Finger is the whole, unprocessed form of turmeric before it's ground into powder. It has a earthy, slightly bitter taste with a warm, peppery aroma. The bright orange-yellow color comes from curcumin, the active compound responsible for turmeric's health benefits.

100% natural and organic
No additives or preservatives
Rich in curcumin (3-5%)
Sun-dried for maximum freshness

Storage Instructions

Store in an airtight container in a cool, dry place away from direct sunlight. Properly stored, turmeric fingers can last up to 2 years while maintaining their flavor and medicinal properties.

Culinary Uses

Turmeric is a staple in Indian, Middle Eastern, and Southeast Asian cuisines. The fingers can be grated or ground into powder to add color and flavor to dishes:

  • Essential in curry powders and spice blends
  • Adds golden color to rice dishes like biryani and pilaf
  • Used in pickles, chutneys, and marinades
  • Flavors soups, stews, and lentil dishes
  • Colors and flavors mustards and other condiments

Medicinal & Other Uses

Turmeric has been used in Ayurvedic and traditional medicine for centuries due to its anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties:

  • Natural anti-inflammatory supplement
  • Supports joint health and mobility
  • Boosts immune system function
  • Used in face masks for glowing skin
  • Natural fabric dye (produces bright yellow color)

Origin & Cultivation

Turmeric (Curcuma longa) is native to Southeast Asia and the Indian subcontinent. India is the largest producer, consumer, and exporter of turmeric in the world, accounting for nearly 80% of global production.

Growing Regions

The best quality turmeric comes from these Indian regions:

  • Alleppey (Kerala) - known for high curcumin content
  • Erode (Tamil Nadu) - "Turmeric City" of India
  • Sangli (Maharashtra) - major production center
  • Nizamabad (Telangana) - known for its deep color
  • Coorg (Karnataka) - organic cultivation

Harvesting Process

Turmeric is typically harvested 7-9 months after planting:

  1. Plants are cut close to the base
  2. Rhizomes are carefully dug up
  3. Fingers are separated from the mother rhizome
  4. They are boiled for 45-60 minutes
  5. Sun-dried for 10-15 days
  6. Polished to remove the outer skin

Climate Requirements

Turmeric thrives in tropical climates with temperatures between 20-35°C (68-95°F) and annual rainfall of 1500mm or more. It grows best in well-drained sandy or clayey loam soils with a pH range of 4.5-7.5.
