Description

Ajwain (Trachyspermum ammi), also known as carom seeds, are small, oval-shaped seeds with a bitter, pungent taste and strong aroma similar to thyme. Our premium quality ajwain seeds are carefully sourced from the best farms in Rajasthan, India, known for producing the most aromatic and flavorful ajwain.

100% natural with no additives
Rich in thymol (active compound)
High essential oil content (2-4%)
Excellent digestive properties

Specifications

Color: Light brown to dark brown

Moisture: Max 10%

Foreign Matter: Max 1%

Size: 1.5-2.5mm

Packaging: 5kg, 10kg, 25kg bags

Shelf Life: 12-18 months

Storage Instructions

Store in a cool, dry place away from direct sunlight and moisture. Keep in airtight containers to maintain freshness and prevent loss of essential oils. Ideal storage temperature is below 25°C with relative humidity below 60%.

Culinary Uses

Ajwain seeds are widely used in Indian cuisine and have various culinary applications:

  • Essential in flavoring breads like parathas and puris
  • Used in tempering (tadka) for dals and curries
  • Added to pickles for digestive benefits
  • Flavoring agent in snacks and fried foods
  • Used in spice blends like panch phoron
  • Flavoring for biscuits and savory baked goods

Medicinal & Other Uses

Ajwain has been used in traditional medicine for centuries due to its therapeutic properties:

  • Relief from indigestion and flatulence
  • Natural remedy for cold and cough
  • Used in Ayurvedic preparations
  • Essential oil used in aromatherapy
  • Antimicrobial properties
  • Used in herbal tooth powders

Origin & Cultivation

Ajwain is believed to have originated in the eastern Mediterranean region, possibly Egypt, and was introduced to India through the Middle East. It has been cultivated in India for thousands of years and is now primarily grown in Rajasthan, Gujarat, and Maharashtra.

Growing Regions

The best quality ajwain comes from these regions:

  • Rajasthan, India - produces 70% of India's ajwain
  • Gujarat, India - known for high essential oil content
  • Maharashtra, India - organic cultivation
  • Iran - traditional growing region
  • Egypt - original native region

Cultivation Process

Ajwain is cultivated as a winter crop in India:

  1. Sowing in October-November
  2. Requires well-drained sandy loam soil
  3. Minimal irrigation needed
  4. Harvesting in February-March
  5. Threshing to separate seeds
  6. Sun drying before packaging
